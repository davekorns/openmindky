Introduction

What is the OpenMind Platform? What Problem Does it Solve?

"Morality binds people into groups. It gives us tribalism, it gives us genocide, war, and politics. But it also gives us heroism, altruism, and sainthood." - Jonathan Haidt

So you want to reverse our nation's polarization and improve civil discourse. It's not going to happen by just encouraging people to be kinder to each other. It's only going to happen when large numbers of people achieve some level of intellectual humility and mutual understanding across their differences. When we get there,  then being kinder and improved discourse will come naturally. 

So you want to achieve intellectual humility and mutual understanding in our society. Again, "How?" you're asking. The bad news is this is a problem of the mind and we have only our minds to study and solve the problem. That seems recursive. The good news is we have a rapidly maturing field of science in Social Psychology and we have the OpenMind Project within the New York University Stern School of Business. 

You may have heard of Heterodox Academy which is also based at NYU and houses the OpenMind Project. While Heterodox Academy focuses on viewpoint diversity within our institutions of higher learning, the OpenMind Project is providing tools and solutions for use on campuses, companies, organizations, and communities.

The key tool is the online interactive course referred to as the OpenMind Platform. Rather than describe it here, visit the OpenMind Plaform website and read their homepage. Then come back here to learn more and begin thinking about how we'll implement this in Kentucky.

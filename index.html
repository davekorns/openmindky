Kentucky is Growing OpenMinds

The Problem

Today's world leads to an unhealthly tendancy to view our community as "us" and "them".

It can hinder potentially fruitful relationships and worst it can inhibit communications and unintentionally isolate people. 

We've simply not been trained to engage constructively with those with different points of view. We talk about the need to be more civil, but how?

The Solution

There is now a course, available to us all, that helps people foster intellectual humility and mutual understanding. It's called the OpenMind™ Platform.

It is a psychology-based course that helps people foster intellectual humility and mutual understanding.

It equips them with the skills to more constructively engage with others.

Behind OpenMindKy.org

Dave Korns, a resident of Greensburg Kentucky, is the administrator of the OpenMindKY.org website. While primarily a software engineer, Dave became interested in Moral Foundation Theory and Cognitive Behavior Theory after being exposed to the work of social psychologist Jonathan Haidt around 2008 (and later his 2012 book: The Righteous Mind: Why Good People Are Divided by Politics and Religion).

While watching today's political climate on television and social media I've often said to myself, "We never get trained in how to understand and engage with people on the other side." It's not easy and I still have to work to make myself better at it.

When I learned about the OpenMind Platform course I was triggered to do something about making as many educators as possible aware of the OpenMind course. So I've created this website and am proactively attempting to reach out to those who might see the value of the course and help implement it. I'm focusing on K-12 and High Ed in the state of state of Kentucky.

Feel free to contact me with questions, requests for assistance to to setup an onsite session where I can present to you, or your group, to better understand the course material and how to deploy it.
